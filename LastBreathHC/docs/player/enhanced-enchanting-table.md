# Player Guide: Enhanced Enchanting Table

The **Enhanced Enchanting Table** is an upgraded enchant station that lets you turn stored enchanted books into a guaranteed enchant roll.

If your shelves are set up right, the bottom enchant option always gives a real, legal enchant instead of a random dud.

---

## What makes it special

- It looks like a normal enchanting table item, but has a custom name: **Enhanced Enchanting Table**.
- The **bottom offer** in the enchant UI becomes a guaranteed result when valid enchant books are nearby.
- That guaranteed option always costs **50 levels**.
- It reads enchantments from **enchanted books inside nearby chiseled bookshelves**.

---

## Crafting recipe

Shape:

```text
S E S
E T E
S B S
```

Ingredients:

- `S` = Nether Star
- `E` = Dragon Egg
- `T` = Enchanting Table
- `B` = Chiseled Bookshelf

---

## How to use it (quick setup)

1. Place your Enhanced Enchanting Table.
2. Place chiseled bookshelves around it in standard enchanting-table positions (same ring/height pattern as normal shelf power spots).
3. Put **enchanted books** into those chiseled bookshelves.
4. Add the item you want to enchant.
5. Pick the bottom offer (50 levels) for the guaranteed enchant pull.

---

## How enchant selection works

The table checks nearby valid chiseled bookshelf slots and builds a pool of candidate enchants.

From that pool, it will:

- Only use enchants that can legally apply to your item.
- Respect normal incompatibility/conflict rules.
- Keep the highest level found for each enchant across your shelves.
- Resolve conflicts by choosing one enchant from each conflicting group.

That means you still get balanced, legal outcomes—but with much more control than vanilla enchanting.

---

## Important rules players should know

- **No valid book enchants nearby = no guaranteed enchant.**
- If the table cannot find valid options for your item, enchanting is cancelled and you get a warning message.
- Breaking an Enhanced Enchanting Table drops the **same custom table item**, so you can move it safely.
- The table identity is tracked per placed block, so normal enchanting tables are unaffected.

---

## Best practices for strong results

- Build themed shelf sets (e.g., sword shelves, armor shelves, tool shelves).
- Keep incompatible enchants separated across stations when possible.
- Upgrade shelf books over time—higher-level books become your new cap for that enchant.
- Save 50 levels before big crafts so you can chain predictable upgrades.
